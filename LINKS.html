More than 16.6 million people have recovered.[8] Common symptoms include fever, cough, fatigue, shortness of breath or breathing difficulties, and loss of smell and taste.[12][5][6][13] While most people have mild symptoms, some people develop acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) possibly precipitated by cytokine storm,[14] multi-organ failure, septic shock, and blood clots.[15][16][17] The time from exposure to onset of symptoms is typically around five days, but may range from two to fourteen days.[5][18]
